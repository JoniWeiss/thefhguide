Goal A1: Learn about U.S. records for your research.

Getting a firm grasp of the basic types of records used in research will help you make better choices in the areas you explore.

Choices

A Understand United States records and their uses.

  1. Learn to gather key pieces of information from records.
  2. Select the record types you need in Ancestry, and discover common limitations of records.
  3. Read this article for tips on beginning your U.S. research.
  4. Explore the United States page of the GenealogyInTime website. Record collections are listed and described in order from newest to oldest additions, including FamilySearch, Ancestry, and many other sources.

B Become familiar with various types of index records.

  1. Learn about the United States Public Records Index . This collection has records for many people who lived in the United States from 1970-2009, which can be a great help for those tracing your recent ancestors or missing family members.
  2. Explore the U.S. Public Records Index to find information and clues about recent ancestors.
  3. Recognize index-only records and learn how to use them in your research.
  4. Learn more about indexes and how to use them.

C Use the Record Selection Table for planning U.S. research.

    This tool helps you determine which records to search first and which to search afterwards, for each category of information.
    For example, if you were looking for a U.S. ancestor's birthplace, you would start with vital records, church records, and census records; then you would try newspapers and obituaries.

  1. Examine a copy of the U.S. Records Selection Table.

D Get acquainted with other types of records for your research.

  1. Explore the FamilySearch Research Wiki to see the various types of records it has to offer. Note: Many of these record types are discussed later in this Project. |
  2. Watch this video to help you explore the Ancestry Wiki, which points to many types of records.
  3. Explore 20 family history resources in this Findmypast article.
  4. Identify the available records for where your ancestor lived.
  5. Use the Record Hints and Research Help features in FamilySearch to find additional record types. Try the tips in this article on ancestors in your Family Tree.

  1. Learn about the type of information contained in birth records.

  1. Click the Search menu and choose Records.

  1. In the Ancestry Menu bar, choose Search and then click Birth, Marriage, & Death.
  2. Fill in the search fields and click Search; or click Show Advanced for additional fields, and click Search.
  3. Fill in the search fields and click Search; or click Show Advanced for additional fields, and click Search.

Choices

A Explore early American and colonial immigration.

  1. Watch this video for ideas on tracing British immigrant ancestors.
  2. Watch these videos to learn more about early immigration to the American colonies. |

B Learn about other immigration records.

  1. Learn about alien registration records in this About.com article.
  2. Learn about passport applications in this Ancestry article.

C Explore the RootsMapper website to visually trace the immigration paths of your ancestors.

  1. Go to www.rootsmapper.com.
  2. Click the blue login button.
  3. Log in to FamilySearch and then click Accept so RootsMapper can use your Family Tree information.
  4. Click Start and select the number of generations to display (default is 3). Ancestors appear as colored dots, with the number inside matching the number of generations before you.
  5. Drag the map and follow the colored lines to see migration paths. Zoom in (+) to add space between ancestors.
