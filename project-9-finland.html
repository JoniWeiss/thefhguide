Goal 1: Learn about Finland research and records.


Choices:


A. Get started with Finnish research.

  1. Use the Finnish Research: Searching Records article to help you with beginning Finnish research.
  2. Explore the Tips for Beginners article for tips on Finnish research.

B. Explore databases in the Genealogical Society of Finland site.

  1. Read the instructions for doing searches in the HisKi database. This site includes records for births, marriages, burial records, etc.
  2. Access the link for the database on the right side of the page.
  3. In the Regions box, select a province from the map or a resource from the the drop-down list at the bottom.
  4. Double-click an area in the left pane to search there, OR click "All" to search all areas in the Region you chose.
  5. Click links on the screen to explore corresponding record sources.

C. Learn about Finnish genealogical terms and surnames.

  1. Learn about Finnish genealogy terms in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Learn about Finland surnames in this FamilySearch Wiki article.

Goal 9: Search records from Finland.


Choices:

A. Use links to quickly search the most common record collections for Finland.

Note: To search records on these sites, you must be registered or be at a FamilySearch Center.

  1. Click a link in the first section to search a FamilySearch collection.
  2. Click a link in the second section to search an Ancestry collection.

B. Explore additional record collections.

  1. 1. Explore the FamilySearch page for Finnish online record collections.
  2. In each program (FamilySearch, Ancestry, Findmypast or MyHeritage), explore additional record collections besides the ones listed in QUIKLinks on this page.

C. Explore GenealogyInTime for Finnish records.
