Goal 4: Learn about Argentina emigration and immigration.


Choices:

A. Learn about Argentine emigration and immigration.

  1. Learn about Argentina emigration and immigration in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Learn about British settlers in Argentina and Uruguay.
  3. Explore Argentine immigration in the CEMLA online database.
  4. To search for ancestors with Italian surnames who moved to Argentina, use the Apellidos Italianos site.
  5. Explore various European migrations to Argentina in the website section of the Wiki page.
  6. Learn about Welsh immigration to the Patagonia region of Argentina.
  7. Explore the Entrada de Pasajeros database for Argentine passenger records.
  8. Explore the Immigrant Ship site. Select a Volume, then choose Argentina as the departure or arrival location.

Goal 9: Search records from Argentina.


Choices:

A. Use links to quickly search the most common record collections for Argentina.

Note: To search records on these sites, you must be registered or be at a FamilySearch Center.

  1. Click a link in the first section to search a FamilySearch collection.
  2. Click a link in the second section to search an Ancestry collection.

B. Explore additional record collections.

  1. Explore the GenArgentina website for research resources.
  2. In each program (FamilySearch, Ancestry, Findmypast or MyHeritage), explore additional record collections besides the ones listed in QUIKLinks on this page.

C. Explore GenealogyInTime for Argentine records.

  1. Explore the South America page of the GenealogyInTime website. Record collections are listed and described in order from newest to oldest additions, including FamilySearch, Ancestry, and many other sources.

Goal 10: Get Help


Choices:

A. Use Facebook pages to get answers to your questions.

  1. For Spanish-speaking, visit Investigacion Genealogica en Argentina.
  2. For English-speaking, visit Hispanic Genealogy Research.

B. Join a mailing list or message board for Argentina research.

  1. Explore Argentina research mailing lists on RootsWeb.
  2. Explore the Argentina research mailing lists on Cyndi's List.
  3. Use the RootsWeb message boards to read or post questions about Argentina research.

C. Learn about Argentina genealogy societies.
