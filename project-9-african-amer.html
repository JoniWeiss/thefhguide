Goal 3: Explore slavery resources for your African-American ancestors.


Choices:

A. Learn about the basic records that document slaves.

  1. Read this article for an introduction to researching slavery records.
  2. Learn about African slave trade ships and manifests in this About.com article.
  3. Explore the Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade Database on the SlaveVoyages.org site.
  4. Watch this video to learn about census, probate, and land records for slaves.
  5. Read this article to learn about U.S. military records for African-Americans.
  6. Explore the AfriGeneas slave record collection.
  7. Learn about the 1860 and 1850 Slave Schedules.

B. Learn about the Freedmen's Bank and Freedmen's Bureau.

  1. Read this article for an introduction to the Freedmen's Bank, a government savings institution where Civil War soldiers and former slaves could invest money.
  2. Read these articles for an introduction to the Freedmen's Bureau, a government services institution that assisted freed people and refugees after the Civil War. |
  3. Go to the Search Records page on FamilySearch and enter "Freedmen's" in the Find a Collection search box to access various Freedmen's Bureau record collections.
  4. Participate in the Freedman's Bureau indexing project for African-American freed slaves.

C. Learn about the National Underground Railroad.

  1. Watch this video for ideas on tracing ancestors who were a part of the Underground Railroad.
  2. Explore the Freedom Center site for the National Underground Railroad.
