Goal 7: Search records from Jamaica.


Choices:

A. Use the links to quickly search the most common record collections for Jamaica.

Note: To search records on these sites, you must be registered or be at a FamilySearch Center.

  1. Click a link in the first section to search a FamilySearch collection.
  2. Click a link in the first section to search an Ancestry collection.

B. Explore additional record collections.

  1. In each program (FamilySearch or Ancestry), explore additional record collections besides the ones listed in QUIKLinks on this page.
  2. Explore the Jamaican Genealogical Society of America site for record resources.
  3. Explore the Jewish Records Indexing project for Jamaica.
  4. Learn about tracing Jewish roots in Jamaica.

C. Explore GenealogyInTime for Jamaican records.

  1. Explore the Caribbean page of the GenealogyInTime website. Record collections are listed and described in order from newest to oldest additions, including FamilySearch, Ancestry, and many other sources.

Goal 8: Get help with your Jamaican research.

Choices:

A. Use Facebook pages to get answers to your questions.

  1. Visit the Jamaica/West Indies Genealogy group on Facebook.
  2. Visit the Jamaica Genealogy Resources group on Facebook.
  3. Visit the Jamaica Genealogy and Diaspora group on Facebook.

B. Join a mailing list or message board for Jamaica research.

  1. Search the RootsWeb site for Jamaica research mailing lists.
  2. Use the RootsWeb message boards to read or post questions about Jamaica research.

C. Learn about Jamaica genealogy societies.

  1. Learn about Jamaica genealogy societies in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Explore Jamaica genealogy society sites on Cyndi's list.
