Goal 3: Learn about Australian immigration and convict records.


Choices:

A. Learn about Australian immigration.

  1. Learn about emigration and immigration in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Explore Australia's Virtual Immigration Wall of Honour site for contact information about Australian immigrants.
  3. Explore shipping lists for Australian immigrants.
  4. Explore the Immigrant Ship site. Select a Volume, then choose Australia as the departure or arrival location.

B. Learn about Australian naturalization and citizenship.

  1. Learn about Australian naturalization and citizenship in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Explore the CoraWeb site for Australian naturalization resources.

C. Learn about Australian convict records.

  1. Learn about convict records in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Explore the Australian English Genealogy site for background on convict research in Australia.
  3. Explore the Convicts Research page in the Janet Reakes Genealogy site.
  4. Explore the Convicts Australia page in Genseek Genealogy.
  5. Explore the CoraWeb site for Australian convict resources.

Goal 10: Search records from Australia.


Choices:

A. Use links to quickly search the most common record collections for Australia.

Note: To search records on these sites, you must be registered or be at a FamilySearch Center.

  1. Click a link in the first section to search a FamilySearch collection.
  2. Click a link in the second section to search an Ancestry collection.

B. Explore additional record collections.

  1. Explore the FamilySearch page for Australian online record collections
  2. In each program (FamilySearch, Ancestry, Findmypast or MyHeritage), explore additional record collections besides the ones listed in QUIKLinks on this page.

C. Explore New South Wales records

  1. Learn about births, deaths, and marriages in New South Wales.
  2. Learn about church records in New South Wales, from 1788-1866.

D. Explore GenealogyInTime for Australian records.

  1. Explore the Australia page of the GenealogyInTime website. Record collections are listed and described in order from newest to oldest additions, including FamilySearch, Ancestry, and many other sources.

Goal 11: Get Help


Choices:

A. Use Facebook pages to get answers to your questions.

  1. Access the Australian Genealogy Group on Facebook.
  2. See the Genealogy on Facebook list of Facebook groups dedicated to helping with research for Australia.

B. Join a mailing list or message board for Australian research.

  1. Join the RootsChat forum.
  2. Search the RootsWeb site for Australian research mailing lists.
  3. Explore the Australian research mailing lists on Cyndi's List.
  4. Use the RootsWeb message boards to read or post questions about Australia research.

C. Learn about Australia genealogy societies.

  1. Learn about Australia genealogy societies in this FamilySearch Wiki article.
  2. Explore Australia genealogy society sites on Cyndi's list.
